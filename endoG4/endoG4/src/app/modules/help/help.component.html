 Citation

EndoQuad: a comprehensive genome-wide experimentally validated endogenous G-quadruplex database.

Sheng Hu Qian, Meng-Wei Shi, Yu-Li Xiong, Yuan Zhang, Ze-Hao Zhang, Xue-Mei Song, Xin-Yin Deng, Zhen-Xia Chen*

Nucleic Acids Research, 2023: gkad966. https://doi.org/10.1093/nar/gkad966

 Introduction

G-quadruplexes (G4s) are four-stranded non-B DNA structures formed in Guanine-rich regions, emerging as the key regulators in gene expression regulation and playing critical roles in development, disease, and tumorigenesis. However, a comprehensive identification of endogenous G4s (eG4s) is still lacking and thus systematic characterization of eG4s in the genome often use predicted quadruplex sequences (PQSs) an alternative. Sporadic studies provided a wealth of G4 ChIP-seq data or experimentally validated eG4s with limited number, however, none collects complete information about eG4s in the genome. To this end, we collected all public available G4 ChIP-seq (1,180) and G4 CUT&Tag (27) samples and acquired 808 Tb data from human, mouse, and chicken, including 45 tissues/cell lines. These data resulted in a total of 391,503, 71,791, and 45,044 eG4s in humans, mice, and chicken, respectively. To help the researchers easily browse and access the data, we developed a comprehensive and interactive EndoQuad web resource. In this database, the users can query the histone modification of eG4s, the interplay between eG4s and Transcription factors, and colocolization of eG4s with disease/cancer associated SNPs/eQTLs. The EndoQuad data and resulting principle provided here could serve as a valuable resource for understanding the function of eG4s and further exploring their regulatory mechanisms and advancing the identification of therapeutic targets.

Function modules
  1. eG4-Group: In this module, users can browse eG4s using gene id or chromosome location. The detailed information of each eG4 was exhibited, including length, structural stability, group, conservations (PhastCons, PhyloP), and its closest gene. In the right panel, User can specify a region to search.
  2. eG4-Epigenetics: Users can explore the histone modification and chromatin accessibility proximal to eG4s by interest samples.
  3. eG4-TF: In this section, we analyzed the regulation of transcription factors (TFs) at eG4 loci and provided the binding sites of each TF.
  4. eG4-SNP/eQTL: In this module, we showed the co-localization of eG4s with disease-associated SNPs and potentially regulatory eQTL in normal and cancer text.
  5. Predicted G4: Users can browse all G4s predicted by pqsfinder.
  6. Prediction: Users can predict PQSs in the any sequence of interest with custom parameters.
 Data collection and process

Data collection

To profile the native eG4s, we performed an in situ eG4 mapping method, G4 CUT&Tag. Briefly, we purified the eG4 structure-specific scFv antibody BG4 and combined BG4 with Tn5-based CUT&Tag approach. In this protocol, eG4 structures are specifically recognized by BG4 in situ and eG4-containing DNA fragments are cut and released by Tn5 transposome-assisted tagmentation. To exhaustively identify eG4s and generate a comprehensive compendium of eG4s across mammals, we searched “Quadruplex” for all public G4 ChIP-seq and Cut&Tag data (N = 1207) from NCBI GEO DataSets and EMBL-EBI. We extracted metadata information of ChIP-seq samples.

Data processing

  1. Quality control: raw fastq data was extracted from sra using fastq-dump. FastQC (v0.11.9) and Trim Galore (v0.6.0) was used to quality control and to get clean data, respectively.
  2. Alignment and Extraction of high-quality reads: We applied bowtie2 (v2.3.5.1) to read alignment for ChIP-seq and ATAC-seq samples and HISAT2 (v2.1.0) to quantification of RNA-seq data. Genome and annotation files for human and mouse were downloaded from GENCODE with the version of hg19 and mm10, respectively. Genome and annotation files for chicken were downloaded from ENSEMBL (v104). For precise quantification and analysis, we only retained high-quality reads with the mapping scores (MAPQ) higher than 20 by SAMtools view (v1.3.1, options: -Sb -q 20)
  3. Peak calling and quantification: After the filtering, for ChIP-seq and ATAC-seq data, duplicates were marked and removed using Picard MarkDuplicates (v2.25.7), and genomic regions (“peaks”) of signal enrichment were called for each sample using MACS2 (v2.2.7.1) subcommand callpeak. For data with biological replicates, peak regions were considered positive if confirmed at least two replicates. All human-confirmed peaks were concatenated. For RNA-seq data, gene level expression were calculated using featureCounts (v2.0.3) and measured using transcripts per kilobase million (TPM).
  4. Download of public data: Epigenomics data from NIH Roadmap Project were obtained from the data portal. Transcription factor (TF) enrichment analysis were obtained from ChIP-ATLAS. The detailed location of TFs were downloaded from GTRD. The 100-way phastCons and phyloP files were downloaded from UCSC genome browser. List of GWAS SNPs was downloaded from the National Human Genome Research Institute's (NHGRI) GWAS catalog. The data about eQTLs in normal tissues was obtained from GTEx and eQTLs in tumors acorss 33 cancer types were downloaded from PancanQTL.
